THE PROBLEM: Our challenge with Iguanas was to push ourselves as a company and develop a new business lead. Iguana’s is a local bar frequented mainly by university students. We saw an opportunity in this company for a re-branding campaign. Being students ourselves, we knew we had the experience and expertise to improve their business and ultimately their messaging. After a month of strategic planning and creative development we went for it and approached the Iguana’s management team with our first new business pitch. We challenged ourselves to create a whole new concept for Iguanas. We truly believed in the work that we were bringing forward and knew that by the end of our pitch, Iguana’s would be head over heels.

THE SOLUTION: Through our strategic planning and collaboration, we created a new brand identity to set Iguana’s apart from other bars. The competition for Iguana’s is tough because they are situated directly next to other bars frequented by the same target audience as Iguana’s. We knew that Iguana’s had a unique product to offer, yet their message was lost with the rest. Our team understood Iguana’s target market and truly got into the mindset of the Iguana’s consumer. We created a twitter account and transferred a Facebook page to a Fan page. This change allowed us to gain new insight and analytics. Along with changing their social presence, we changed their visual presence as well. The creative pieces we have delivered thus far include; new logos, menus, collateral pieces, t-shirts, bar toppers for special events and holidays. We continue to work with Iguana’s today. Next steps include building a unique website for the local bar.

THE PROBLEM: Nebraska is known for their wide-open spaces, and The Nebraska Dairy Association wanted to utilize those spaces by showcasing Nebraska as a positive alternative for dairy farming. They wanted to show farmers from across the nation that Nebraska is great place to call home sweet home, and if their needs aren’t being met where they are, Nebraska will welcome them.

THE SOLUTION: We conducted research for the Nebraska Dairy Association showing that Nebraska does makes a beautiful place to raise your cattle and make delicious cheese and milk. We also researched Nebraska’s competition focusing mainly on California. We created analysis of both showing prospective clients the pros to moving their herd to the good life. We discovered that we weren’t just selling the association. We were selling the Nebraska’s personality, and we are pretty darn friendly. Our down home and personable feel worked its way into the designs for their logo, trade show banners, and pamphlets. The pamphlet contained all the research we conducted, from financial information to detailed demographics. In total we created a complete rebranding for them.

THE PROBLEM: The Textile, Clothing, and Design program through the College of Education and Human Sciences was looking to promote their biennial fashion show. They wanted to increase interest of prospective students for the College of Education and Human Sciences as well as the Textile, Clothing, and Design Department. They were ready to show the community that they were an edgy and fashion-forward program that everyone should take notice of. The college wanted to raise awareness of the work coming out of their program to family, friends, designers, alumni, and sponsors. The main goal was to raise awareness of the program, indirectly increase enrollment, and to, of course, to get the show to sell out.

THE SOLUTION: We saw this as an opportunity to flex our creative and social muscles. First, we made a logo and ticket designs. Then, we made our main focus utilizing social media, as we saw this as the most effective way of reaching different audiences. We increased the fashion show’s visibility on Facebook, created the hashtag #UNLfashion on Twitter, and even live-pinned the show on Pinterest. Integrating posts over various social media outlets along with press releases sent to local publications, we created a sold out show. The social media strategy was so outstanding that the college hired two social media interns to keep up with the accounts after the event for recruitment purposes. With all of this promotion we helped Sponsor Tickets, priced at $75, sell out in a week. General tickets also sold out despite the fact that ticket price double from the previous year’s show.

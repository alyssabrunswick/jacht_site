THE PROBLEM: Our challenge with Iguana's was to push ourselves as a company and develop a new business lead. Iguana’s is a local bar frequented mainly by university students. We saw an opportunity in this company for a re-branding campaign. Being students ourselves, we knew we had the experience and expertise to improve their messaging and ultimately their business. After a month of strategic planning and creative development we approached the Iguana’s management team with our first new business pitch. We challenged ourselves to create a whole new concept for Iguana's. We truly believed in the work that we were bringing forward and knew that by the end of our pitch, Iguana’s would be head over heels.

THE SOLUTION: Through our strategic planning and collaboration, we created a new brand identity to set Iguana’s apart from other bars. The competition for Iguana’s is tough because they are situated directly next to other bars frequented by the same target audience. We knew that Iguana’s had a unique product to offer, yet their message was lost with the rest. Our team understood Iguana’s demographics and got into the mindset of the Iguana’s consumer. We created a Twitter account and transferred their Facebook page to a Fan page. This change allowed us to gain new insight and analytics. Along with changing their social presence, we changed their visual presence as well. The creative pieces we have delivered include: new logos, menus, collateral pieces, t-shirts, bar toppers for special events and holidays. We continue to work with Iguana’s today. Our next steps include building a unique website for the bar.

THE PROBLEM: Nebraska is known for its wide-open spaces, and The Nebraska Dairy Association wanted to utilize those spaces by showcasing Nebraska as a positive alternative to other regions for dairy farming. They wanted to show farmers from across the nation that Nebraska is great place to call home, and if their needs aren’t being met where they are, Nebraska will welcome them.

THE SOLUTION: We conducted research for the Nebraska Dairy Association showing that Nebraska makes a beautiful place to raise your cattle and make delicious cheese and milk. We also researched Nebraska’s competition, focusing mainly on California. We discovered that we weren’t just selling the association. We were selling Nebraska’s personality, and we are pretty darn friendly. Our down-home and personable feel worked its way into the designs for their logo, trade show banners, and pamphlets. The pamphlet contained all the research we conducted, from financial information to detailed demographics. In total, we created a complete rebranding for them.

THE PROBLEM: The Textile, Clothing, and Design program through the College of Education and Human Sciences at the University of Nebraska was looking to promote their biennial fashion show. They wanted to increase interest of prospective students in the College of Education and Human Sciences as well as the Textile, Clothing, and Design Department. They were ready to show the community that they were an edgy and fashion-forward program. The College wanted to raise awareness of the work coming out of their program to family, friends, designers, alumni, and sponsors. The main goal was to raise awareness of the program, indirectly increase enrollment, and, of course, to get the show to sell out.

THE SOLUTION: We saw this as an opportunity to flex our creative and social muscles. First, we made a logo and designed tickets. Then we made our main focus utilizing social media, as we saw this as the most effective way of reaching different audiences. We increased the fashion show’s visibility on Facebook, created the hashtag #UNLfashion on Twitter, and even live-pinned the show on Pinterest. By integrating posts over various social media platforms along with press releases sent to local publications, we created a sold-out show. The social media strategy was so outstanding that the college hired two social media interns to keep up with the accounts after the event for recruitment purposes. With all of this promotion, we helped Sponsor Tickets, priced at $75, sell out in a week. General tickets also sold out, despite the fact that ticket price doubled from the previous year.

THE PROBLEM: Speedway Motors saw an opportunity to help their racing customers cross the finish line sooner. Their customers were struggling to keep all of their information organized. From their parts to their records, these dirt-track speed demons had multiple things to juggle. They were sifting through papers and the deep crevices of their minds to find the chassis setups and race times they needed. Speedway saw this as a chance to help out their customers and create something they can use and access wherever they go.

THE SOLUTION: With UNL’s Raikes School, we worked to create a web and mobile application. This app would be able to be accessed anywhere, at any time and contain all the information they would need to race at their maximum potential. Their mobile application utilizes GPS tracking along with a stopwatch feature to record race times. We worked with Speedway to better understand racers and make their products as racer-friendly as possible. To do that, we did research on their competitors as well as their customers. We helped them set up user interface testing and pricing. In the end, development happened quickly enough to allow Speedway to move their estimated beta release date up by more than a year.

THE PROBLEM: Partnership for Healthy Lincoln came to us with a problem affecting our local community: they wanted to stomp out childhood obesity. The culprit they saw as a worthy adversary to defeat was the dastardly sugary beverages everyone loves to consume. From pop to sugar-laced juices, Partnership for Healthy Lincoln wanted to knock those choices out of the hands and minds of Lincolnites. The Partnership wanted to reach Lincoln residents, from kids to adults, right in their own homes.

THE SOLUTION: Partnership for Healthy Lincoln originally asked us to create a public service announcement to help Lincoln realize what they were putting into their bodies. Through analyzing research that The Partnership provided to us we determined that most people are unaware of the health facts that go along with these drinks. The key insight we found was that people were more willing to put hundreds of calories of sugary drinks into their bodies than eat the same amount of calories in cookies and cakes. With that in mind, we created three concepts for PSAs for them to choose from, each contrasting the amount of sugar in drinks and deserts. The Partnership was so pleased with their choices that they chose to feature all three on local television channels.

THE PROBLEM: Nebraska’s Shrine Bowl is an opportunity for high school athletes to shine one last time. The premiere high school senior football players from across the state play in this exhibition game to support Shriners Hospitals. The Shrine Bowl had no problem racking up good karma, but they wanted more people to partake in the festivities. They previously focused their efforts on attracting the parents of the athletes, but this time they wanted to target high schoolers. Getting students to come out and support their classmates was a big goal. The Shriners wanted to raise ticket sales in general, and, more than that, they wanted to raise awareness for the game, to make Shrine Bowl a more recognized event.

THE SOLUTION: We completely rebranded the Shrine Bowl, giving it a fresh and youthful feel to attract the friends of players. On their request, we created a new logo for the game. That new logo then translated into four different types of tickets designs, posters, and a program cover. A new look alone wasn’t enough to get students out there, so we created a social media plan. We created Twitter and Facebook pages, as well as player bios for their website so that people could feel even more connected to the game by seeing exactly who was playing in it. Those bios were also featured in local newspapers. We created buzz by writing a number of press releases as well as holding a press conference. All of our efforts had a result of increasing ticket sales about 15 percent.
